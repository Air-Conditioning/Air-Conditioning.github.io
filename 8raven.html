The noisy raven

Looking up at the branches of a tree behind you, a raven peers at you with its intelligent eyes. It’s almost as if it's staring through you like it wants something from you.

It caws at you one, two, three times before flap-jumping to another branch. It caws once more. It seems like it wants you to follow.

What do you do?

—> You decide to follow the raven.

—> Your parents told you not to trust strangers. That doesn’t exclude birds. Ignore the raven and check out the cabin instead.
